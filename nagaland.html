About The Tour


Sekrenyi, the most significant festival of the Angami Nagas, is a vibrant celebration of purification, renewal, and unity. Observed during late February in the Angami lunar month of "Kezei," the festival is deeply rooted in the agricultural traditions of the tribe. The villages of Khonoma and Kigwema, situated in the Kohima district, are among the most notable places to experience this cultural extravaganza, where traditions come alive amidst breathtaking landscapes.

Khonoma, renowned as India’s first green village, boasts a rich history of ecological conservation and resistance to British colonization. During Sekrenyi, rituals such as "Dzuseva," a ceremonial bath symbolizing purification and renewal, take center stage. The villagers engage in traditional dances, folk songs, and games, celebrating community bonds and paying homage to their ancestors.

In Kigwema, famous for its traditional Angami architecture, the festival includes rituals like "Thekra Hie," where families and friends gather to share food and stories, strengthening communal ties. Both villages adorn themselves with vibrant colors as villagers don traditional attire, showcasing the intricate artistry of their heritage.
Adding to the cultural richness of the region is its historical significance from World War II. Nagaland, particularly Kohima and its surrounding villages, played a pivotal role during the war. The Battle of Kohima, fought in 1944, was a turning point in the Burma Campaign, marking a decisive victory for the Allied forces over the Japanese. Villages like Khonoma and Kigwema bear silent witness to this history, with war cemeteries and memorials standing as poignant reminders.
Today, Sekrenyi is not just a festival but a bridge connecting the past with the present, blending age-old traditions with the echoes of history, all set against the stunning backdrop of Nagaland’s hills and terraced fields.

Trip highlights



Sekrenyi Festival

The Sekrenyi Festival, celebrated by the Angami Nagas of Nagaland, marks renewal, purification, and community bonding. Held in late February, it begins with the ritual of Dzuseva, a ceremonial bath symbolizing spiritual cleansing. Traditional songs, dances, and games reflect the tribe’s rich cultural heritage. Villages like Khonoma and Kigwema come alive with feasts and vibrant gatherings, fostering unity and joy. Sekrenyi is a time to honor traditions, nature, and ancestral wisdom, offering a unique glimpse into Nagaland’s rich cultural fabric.

Kigwema

Kigwema, a historic village near Kohima, is one of the oldest settlements of the Angami Naga tribe. Its name, meaning "inheritance of the house," reflects the community's deep ancestral roots. Known for its traditional Angami architecture and rich oral history, Kigwema played a significant role as a shelter for Indian National Army troops during World War II. The village is a hub of cultural heritage, where festivals like Sekrenyi are celebrated with vibrant rituals, dances, and feasts.

Khonoma

Khonoma, often called India’s first green village, is a historic Angami Naga settlement, known for its eco-conscious practices and resistance to colonial rule. It was a key site of the Angami-British wars in the 19th century, symbolizing Naga resilience. The village preserves its rich culture through traditional festivals like Sekrenyi, intricate terraced farming, and indigenous craftsmanship. Steeped in history and cultural pride, Khonoma offers a glimpse into the sustainable and resilient lifestyle of the Angami Nagas.

Nagaland weaves and handicrafts

Nagaland is renowned for its vibrant handwoven textiles and intricate handicrafts, reflecting the cultural diversity of its tribes. The traditional Naga shawls, adorned with bold geometric patterns and symbolic motifs, represent tribal identity and status. Skilled artisans craft exquisite bamboo and cane products, including baskets, mats, and furniture, showcasing sustainable practices. Wood carving and beadwork are other prominent crafts, with intricate designs depicting folklore and tribal heritage.

Kohima War Cemetery

The Kohima War Cemetery is a poignant memorial honoring soldiers who fought in the Battle of Kohima during World War II in 1944. Maintained by the Commonwealth War Graves Commission, it is the resting place of over 1,420 Allied soldiers who lost their lives in the decisive battle against Japanese forces. The cemetery’s centerpiece is the inscription: “When you go home, tell them of us and say, ‘For your tomorrow, we gave our today.’” Located on Garrison Hill, the site stands as a tribute to bravery, sacrifice, and peace.

Trip itinerary




Day 1: Arrive in Dimapur, Drive to Kohima

Arrive at Dimapur by 12 noon.
- Pickup from Dimapur airport.
- Visit the Kachari ruins which date back to 10 th century . Over here you will be able to see the mushroom domed pillars built by the Kachari kings who predated the Ahoms.
- Thereafter continue your drive to Kohima , the vibrant capital of Nagaland .

Overnight @ Kohima.

Day 2: Kigwema

- Today we will explore Kigwema village , located on the foot of Mt. Japfu the second highest mountain in Nagaland. Kigwema which loosely translates to “People who inherit houses” is one of the oldest Angami villages with a significant role in World War II . Walking along its paths will give you an idea of the culture and history of this village.
- From here continue on to the World War II cemetery, built to commemorate all those who lost their lives in the epic World War II Battle of Kohima. Later in the evening explore the Kohima market where you can also buy some handicrafts.

Overnight @ Kohima.

Day 3: Khonoma excursion

- Today we will drive to the village of Khonoma, one of the most beautiful villages in all of Nagaland. This village inhabited by the Angami tribe has a very rich history and culture . Walk around the village interacting with the locals and understanding the culture of the Angami society.
- On the way back drive to Jotsoma village where we will have a cultural performance. By evening drive back to Kohima.

Overnight @ Kohima.

Day 4: Sekrenyi festival

- Today we will head to a village where they will celebrate Sekrenyi, the most important festival of the Angami tribe. Sekrenyi is a compound word formed by Sekre meaning "sanctification" and Nyi meaning "festival." The objective of the festival is to renew and "make holy" by cleansing the "body and the soul" of the village as a whole.

Overnight @ Kohima.

Day 5: Kohima to Dimapur

- After breakfast drive to Heirloom Naga Centre in Dimapur where you will be able to learn about the rich weaving culture of the various Naga tribes.
- We will participate in a session where participants will be taught to weave in a hands on session. This will be followed by a traditional lunch.
- In the second half visit the sprawling Dimapur market where you will be able to see the produce from all over Nagaland.

Overnight @ Dimapur.

Day 6: Goodbye!

- After breakfast drop to Dimapur airport.

Location details


Nagaland, a picturesque state in northeast India, is known for its lush hills, valleys, and dense forests. Nestled amidst the Patkai Range, its undulating terrain includes fertile valleys and towering peaks, such as Mount Saramati, the highest point at 3,826 meters. The state enjoys a subtropical monsoon climate with heavy rainfall between May and September, followed by cool, dry winters. Kohima, the state capital, is perched at an altitude of about 1,500 meters. Its temperate climate features pleasant summers and chilly winters, making it an ideal destination year-round. The region's pristine landscapes are rich in biodiversity and agricultural beauty.
