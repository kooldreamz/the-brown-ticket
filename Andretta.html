About The Tour


Back in 1920s, an Irish theatre artist and environmentalist, Norah Richards, made Andretta her home, and started the makeover of the village into an artists’ hub! Located near Palampur in Kangra district, in the backdrop of Dhauladhar Himalayan range, this tiny miny settlement has since then attracted many noted artists, theatre practitioners, painters and more recently potters.

In its early days, travel to the village was not easy. It took a 12-hour train journey, followed by a bus ride, and the last nine miles were covered on foot. Even so, it started attracting artists from all over, especially Lahore. The artists who have made the village their home since its beginning includes - noted painter and sculptor B.C. Sanyal, the theatre meastro Prithvi Raj Kapoor, Sardar Sobha Singh - who defined Sikh religious paintings and many more.

Later on, Sardar Gurucharan Singh, a noted potter who started the Delhi Blue Pottery in 1952, established base here and stayed here as long as he lived. Following his father's foorsteps, his son established the Andretta Pottery and Craft Society, which till date is active with a beautiful production studio! It is to this less explored village that we the TheBrownTicketExperiences take you for a-connect-with yourself experience!

Trip highlights



Masroor rock cut temples

Masroor rock cut temples is a remarkable example of monolithic rock-cut architecture. It features a group of 15 sandstone temples carved out of a single rock. These temples date back to the 8th century and are dedicated to various Hindu deities. Carved meticulously out of a single sandstone rock, this monolithic wonder is often called the “Ellora of Himachal”.

Kangra Fort

The perfect blend of rich history and scenic beauty, the Kangra Fort is said to be the largest hill fort in the Himalayas. With majestic gateways and bastions defending the fort, it boasts of ancient temples, vast courtyards and tales of valour etched in every stone. Add the majestic Dhauladhar ranges in the backdrop of this majestic hill fort and the views are breathtaking.

Wah Tea Estate

At 526 acres, Wah Tea Estate is the largest and the oldest tea estate in Kangra region. Apart from the lush greenery of the estate, the place houses some quirky tea-environment, and a lovely restaurant overlooking green cover of tea plants. Established in 1857 by the British, right now it is very lovingly run by 4th generation of Prakash family.

Meet the Artist

While in an artists’ village, meet one too!Pradeep, the artist who started ClayrVoy studio, is a physicist by education, and after spending many years in Silicon valley, started learning pottery and made Andretta his home since 2021! Get to meet him, talk to him and know about his journey into this totally different tangent!

Sobha Singh Art Gallery

Sardar Sobha Singh is a renowned painter and artist who is best known for defining Sikh Gurus in paintings. His masterpiece of Guru Nanak (with his one hand raised as if to bestow blessings) is till date the defining image of Guru Nanak. He also made Andretta his home, and today his house “Grow More Good” is a small gallery run by his family.

Norah Richards House

Chameli niwas - the house of Norah Richards, the Irish theatre artist who made Andretta her home, and nurtured it to become an artists’ hub. Choosing to live amidst the villagers in Andretta, Norah Richards adopted the same lifestyle. And the thatched roof house she made for herself reflects this. It is a Kangra-style mud house built using local style and materials, using mud, slate, and bamboo.

Andretta Pottery

Sardar Gurucharan Singh, a noted potter who started the Delhi Blue Pottery in the 1952, established base here and stayed here as long as he lived. Later on his son established the Andretta Pottery and Craft Society, which till date is active with a beautiful production studio ! They offer varied grade of pottery classes, starting from 45 minutes to 3 months.

Sherabling Monastery

The main prayer hall of the monastery has a stunning two-storey golden Maitreya statue. Built across a sprawling 3 acres of land, the Sherabling monastery is situated within the Bhatu forest in the foothills of the Himalayan ranges. The path leading to the monastery is full of pine trees on either side and the colorful prayer flags welcome everyone all through the way.

Baijnath temple

Baijnath Temple is a Nagara style Hindu temple situated in Kangra District. Built in early 13th century by two local merchants named Ahuka and Manyuka, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva as Vaidyanath ‘the Lord of physicians’. The temple sees equal number of religious devotees and architecture buffs.


Trip itinerary




Day 1: Arrive in Amb, travel to Kangra

Arrive by noon at Kangra Hotel.
Lunch and briefing @ Hotel
Post lunch we head out to Masroor rock cut temples (35 km/1 hr drive).
Masroor rock cut temples is a remarkable example of monolithic rock-cut architecture. It features a group of 15 sandstone temples carved out of a single rock.
Retire early. Dinner @ Kangra / hotel

Day 2: Andretta, the artists' village

Breakfast @ hotel
Check-out from the hotel, and head out to Kangra Fort, one of the oldest forts in India to get a virtual experience of time travel.
We start our journey to the artists’ village of Andretta, and our next stop is Wah tea estate (40km/1-1.5 hr drive).
Take a stroll around lush green tea estates, and enjoy the quirky tea-environment at the estate. Experience the vast range of tea the estate has to offer in their tea-tasting session.
Enjoy Kangri Dham (the traditional Kangra food platter) at Wah estate for lunch and move to our final destination - Andretta! (7km /15 mins)
Check in to the homestay, freshen up and head out to our “meet the artist” session at ClayrVoy pottery studio over a cup of tea.

Day 3: Tales of Andretta

Breakfast @ homestay Start exploring the small town of Andretta on foot.
Visit Sardar Sobha Singh's art gallery. His family lives in the town, and runs a small museum showcasing his works. Indulge in the artist’s finesse and his career as painter. Chameli niwas - the house of Norah Richards - the woman who gave this town the identity of an artists' hub
Post lunch, we get our hands dirty with clay! We attend a beginner’s pottery session at Andretta Pottery! Let your inner child out and have fun in a 45 minute session, which leaves you yearning for more..
In the evening, enjoy a small cultural evening with local performers.

Day 4: Goodbye

An optional visit to Baijnath temple in early morning.
A mosaic workshop
Visit Sherabling Monastery. (20km/ 45 mins drive).
Post lunch start for the Amb railway station

Location details


Some 30 odd kms from Dharamshala, Andretta is a small artists’ village in Kangra valley. Surrounded by the snow-capped Dhauladhar range and the bamboo groves of the Shivaliks, the village enjoys surreal beauty and pleasant weather throughout the year
