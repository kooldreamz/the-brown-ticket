About The Tour


The bastions and walls of a hill fort play hide and seek as the road snakes around a narrow gorge at the edge of the Aravallis. Soon you see the charming zig-zagging lines of blue-lime-washed houses... You spot a few palace spires and many Baolis (stepwells) brimming with water...a few neat rows of bustling bazaars, full of a motley collection of people and modes of transport... The fragrances of freshly cooked kachoris every now and then, and you can see the towering ramparts of the 12th century hill fort above the town... Welcome to Bundi!

With its beguiling architecture, hill forts and palaces, exquisite bails, serene lakes, and a symphony of colors that dance through its streets, and above all…rock-paintings dating back to ten thousand years ago, Bundi is a captivating gem waiting to be discovered.

As you step foot in this time-honored city, you are transported to an era where kings ruled with opulence and artisans brought stone to life. Where its Baolis echo the whispers of enchanting historic tales. But Bundi is not merely a city of stone and water; it is a canvas upon which nature paints its most vibrant hues too, being situated on one of the most green parts of the Aravallis.

Are you wondering why haven’t you heard too much about Bundi...because the majestic fort town of Bundi seems pretty content being overshadowed by Rajasthan's bigger fort towns! Yet Bundi offers, perhaps one of the most complete travel experiences in Rajasthan...history starting from the prehistoric cave-dwellers to the vast fort-palaces of the Rajputs and Mughals...architectural brilliance of the many gorgeously designed Baolis and Temples, indulge your love of art as you admire the 400-years old wall art fresco paintings, which is world famous as the Bundi School of Miniature Paintings. and experience the raw, natural beauty of the Aravallis amidst the lively colours of Rajasthan, and savoring Rajasthani hospitality, at its simplest, pristine, untouched best!!

Trip highlights



Raniji ki Baori

Raniji ki Baori is a magnificent step-well built in 17th century by Queen Nathavati, the consort of Rao Raja Anirudh Singh. This architectural marvel features intricate carvings and ornate pillars, showcasing the splendid craftsmanship of the era. The step-well, over 46 meters deep, served as a vital water source and a place of refuge during the hot summers.

Garh Palace

Garh Palace is a majestic 17th-century fort complex known for its splendid architecture and intricate murals. The palace, perched on a hillside, features a series of interconnected buildings, courtyards, and gardens, reflecting the grandeur of Rajput architecture. Garh Palace in Bundi comprises several mahals (palaces), each built by different rulers over time.

Taragarh Fort

Taragarh Fort is a majestic 14th-century fort known for its strategic location atop a steep hill. Constructed by Rao Raja Bar Singh, the fort boasts massive battlements, impressive gateways, and a network of tunnels. It features the grand Rani Mahal, which once served as the residence of royal women, and the impressive Bhim Burj, which housed the famous cannon Garbh Gunjam.

Chitrashala

Chitrashala, also known as the Ummed Mahal, is a splendid gallery within Garh Palace in Bundi. It is renowned for its vibrant murals and frescoes depicting scenes from Hindu mythology, court life, and local legends. The intricate artwork, characterised by rich colours and detailed craftsmanship, offers a glimpse into the cultural and artistic heritage of Bundi.

Ancient Rock Paintings

Bundi is renowned for its ancient cave paintings, found primarily in the surrounding hills and rock shelters. These paintings, dating back thousands of years, depict scenes of daily life, hunting, rituals, and religious motifs. They provide insights into the prehistoric cultures and artistic traditions of the region, attracting archaeologists and art enthusiasts seeking to unravel Bundi's rich heritage.

Garadiya Mahadev

Garadia Mahadev Gorge is a breathtaking natural canyon carved by the Chambal River. The gorge offers panoramic views of the river winding through rugged cliffs and lush forests. It is a popular spot for nature lovers and photographers seeking stunning vistas and serene surroundings. Garadia Mahadev Gorge is also revered as a sacred site, attracting both tourists and pilgrims alike.

Sukh Mahal & Jait Sagar Lake

Sukh Mahal is a beautiful palace located on the banks of the Jait Sagar Lake. Built during the reign of Umed Singh, it served as a summer retreat for the royal family, featuring a pleasing blend of Mughal and Rajput architectural styles. The famous British writer Rudyard Kipling spent time at Sukh Mahal in Bundi. During his stay, he was inspired by the beauty and history of the place.

Chaurasi Khamba

Chaurasi Khambha (84 Pillars) is a remarkable cenotaph built in the 17th century. It consists of numerous pillared pavilions surrounding a central courtyard. Each pavilion is intricately carved and commemorates members of the ruling Chauhan dynasty, reflecting the artistic prowess of the era. It serves as a poignant reminder of Bundi's royal past.

Dhabai Kund

Dhabai Kund an impressive step-well renowned for its geometric design and majestic size. Built in the 17th century, this kund features steep steps leading down to the water, with intricately carved niches and pillars adorning its walls. It was constructed to provide a reliable water source and serves as an example of the region's advanced water management techniques.

Bijolia Temple Complex

The Bijoliya Temples, located near Bundi, are a group of ancient Hindu temples. Dating back to the 9th and 12th centuries, these temples feature intricate carvings and sculptures dedicated to Shiva, Vishnu, and other deities. The most notable among them is the Bijolia Shiva Temple, known for its exquisite stone work and detailed iconography.

Location details


Bundi, located in the Hadoti region of Rajasthan, India, is situated on the eastern edge of the Aravalli Range, with the Chambal River flowing nearby. The town is characterized by rocky terrain, hills, and ravines, creating a picturesque landscape. Bundi is known for its stepwells (baoris) which historically provided water for the town.
* Summer (March to June): Summers in Bundi are hot with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F). The weather is dry and scorching, making it less favorable for outdoor activities.
* Monsoon (July to September): Bundi receives moderate rainfall during the monsoon season, transforming the region into a lush green landscape. The rains rejuvenate the surroundings and make this period relatively cooler.
* Winter (October to February): Winters are mild and pleasant, with temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C (50°F to 77°F).
