About The Tour


A Walk In The Clouds in the region where the clouds descend to greet you, a region that is known as the playground of the rain gods, where the greenery is so lush that it enchants you. This is Meghalaya, where the fine line between the sky and earth is lost amid the mist. And what better time to visit this abode high up in the sky than the monsoon! Come out of the rain and walk into the clouds – literally. You will be able to embrace the rainfall and misty rolling hills in all their glory on this trip, when waterfalls rage on in full energy.

The tour will take you on a journey through Meghalaya, from Shillong, the capital of Meghalaya, to Asia’s cleanest village, Mawlynnong , on the border of Bangladesh. You get to travel through Cherrapunji and Mawsynram, the heaviest rainfall zone in the world, thundering waterfalls and underground caves. Get in your gumboots and wear your raincoats and get ready to explore the heart and soul of the monsoon!

Trip highlights



Umiam Lake

Umiam Lake, also known as Barapani, is a picturesque man-made reservoir near Shillong, surrounded by lush pine forests and rolling hills. Originally built for hydroelectric power, it now draws visitors for its scenic beauty, tranquil vibes, and recreational activities. Especially enchanting at sunrise, its misty waters offer a magical introduction to the serene charm of Meghalaya.

Mawphlang

Mawphlang, a scenic village near Shillong, is famous for its ancient sacred groves and rich Khasi heritage. These protected forests are believed to be the dwelling of nature spirits and have been preserved for centuries. Visitors can walk through mossy trails, explore traditional monoliths, and experience the spiritual connection locals maintain with nature in this lush, mystical landscape.

Mawsynram

Mawsynram, a small village in the East Khasi Hills of Meghalaya, holds the title of the wettest place on Earth. It receives record-breaking rainfall, especially during the monsoon season. Surrounded by dramatic cliffs, caves, and lush greenery, Mawsynram offers a surreal experience of nature’s raw power and beauty, making it a must-visit for rain and nature lovers alike.

Laitlum canyons

Laitlum Canyons offer breathtaking panoramic views of Meghalaya’s rolling hills and deep gorges. The name “Laitlum” means “end of the hills” in the local Khasi language. Often wrapped in mist, this serene spot is perfect for nature lovers and photographers. The trek down the canyon reveals remote villages and untouched natural beauty, making it a memorable experience.

Don Bosco museum

The Don Bosco Museum in Shillong is a vibrant showcase of Northeast India's diverse cultures and traditions. Spread across seven floors, it features themed galleries on tribal life, art, music, costumes, and architecture. Its skywalk offers panoramic views of the city. Engaging and educational, the museum is a must-visit to understand the rich heritage of the region’s indigenous communities.

Nohkalikai & other waterfalls

The waterfalls of Meghalaya come alive during the monsoon, cascading down cliffs in spectacular displays of nature’s power. From the majestic Nohkalikai Falls to the crystal-clear Krangshuri and the hidden gem Phe Phe Falls, each offers a unique experience. Surrounded by lush greenery and mist, these waterfalls are among the most breathtaking sights in India’s northeast.

Mawlynnong village

Mawlynnong, often called the “Cleanest Village in Asia,” is a charming Khasi settlement known for its spotless lanes, bamboo dustbins, and eco-conscious community. Surrounded by lush greenery and offering views of the Bangladesh plains, it reflects sustainable living in harmony with nature. Enjoy its living root bridge and soak in warm local hospitality.

Cherrapunji

Cherrapunji, nestled in the East Khasi Hills of Meghalaya, is renowned for its lush landscapes and record-breaking rainfall. Famous for stunning waterfalls like Nohkalikai and dramatic limestone caves, it’s a paradise during the monsoon. The region is also home to the iconic living root bridges. With mist-covered valleys and vibrant greenery, Cherrapunji offers a truly magical experience.

Mawsmai caves

One of Meghalaya’s most popular natural attractions, these limestone caves feature stunning stalactite and stalagmite formations, creating a surreal underground landscape. Well-lit and easily accessible, the caves offer an adventurous yet safe experience for visitors. Their cool, mysterious interiors provide a fascinating glimpse into the region’s geological wonders!

Living Root Bridges

These are unique live bridges found only in the Khasi Hills of Meghalaya. The secondary roots of a species of Indian Rubber Tree Ficus Elastica- are trained over 20-25 years to form these living bridges to cross fast flowing rivers and streams. Some of these bio-engineering wonders are 50 to 100 feet long. They are very strong, can carry many people at a time and perhaps can last 500 years.

Trip itinerary




Day 1: Guwahati to Shillong
114 kms/4 hours

Take a flight that reaches Guwahati airport by 9 am.
- Drive from Lokpriya Gopinath Bordoloi International Airport to the mesmerizing and beautiful abode of clouds and the playground of the rain Gods, Meghalaya.
- By afternoon, you will reach Umiam, also known as Barapani, and have lunch viewing the serene landscape of Umiam Lake surrounded by lush greenery, pine trees and hills.
- Shillong City, just 20 kms ahead of lake Umiam, the busy streets of Police Bazaar and Bara Bazaar will welcome you, filled with festivity throughout the year, with ample options for shoppers and local made culinary and handicrafts.
- As you wind up the gentle slopes of Shillong from Guwahati, you will feel the zing of the cool monsoon air in your lungs, refreshing you and getting you ready for the scattered rains ahead.

O/n at Blueberry inn / Shillong

Day 2: Shillong / Mawphlang / Mawsynram

- In the morning drive to Mawphlang for an enticing forest walk in the sacred groves. The Khasi of Meghalaya at one time were animists who believed in the spirits of the forest. Hence every village maintained a tract of forest called "Sacred Grove" where they used to worship the elements of nature and perform sacrifices.
- The forests of Mawphlang are one of the last surviving sacred groves in Meghalaya.
- Continue on to Mawsynram, currently the wettest place on Earth . In May this road will be lush green with spectacular vistas greeting you at every bend. When the clouds lift up their curtain you will get to see the most stunning views of the Meghalaya plateau from here.

O/n at Blueberry inn / Shillong

Day 3: Shillong / Laitlum canyons

- In the morning drive to Laitlum canyons , from where you can enjoy breath-taking views of the gorge and the valley below.
- Continue on to Don Bosco museum, a six storied ethnographic museum where you will be able to learn about all major tribes from North-East.
- In the second half drive to Bara Bazaar locally known as Iewduh, one of the largest markets in all of North East. The bustling lanes of the market are a photographer's delight and will give you a good sense of the local culture and diversity.
- Later in the afternoon you will go to see a game of ‘Teer’, a local form of archery on which the locals gamble. It is the most loved sport of Meghalaya, with interesting rules and cultural background.
- Later in the day you can visit Wards Lake from where you can walk to Polic bazaar to shop for local handicrafts.

O/n at Blueberry inn / Shillong

Day 4: Shillong – PhePhe – Mawlynnong
148 kms / 4.5 hours

- It’s a waterfall day today. You will take the Jowai road , where you will first visit Phe Phe falls which is around a 30 minute walk from the road head. It is probably the most breathtaking waterfall in the state.
- You will drive a little further to the fabled Krangshuri falls.
- After having your fill, continue on to Dawki, on the Indo Bangladesh border and further on to the village of Mawlynnong.

O/n at Hala Tyngkong / Mawlynnong

Day 5: Mawlynnong

- After breakfast, explore Mawlynnong, the cleanest village in Asia.
- Visit the nearby villages of Riwai and Nowhet.
- In the second half drive to the nearby village of Pynter, known for its Khasi folk music. The village is well known for its art of making Khasi folk instruments. Over here, you will listen to a group of local musicians play traditional folk music.

O/n at Hala Tyngkong / Mawlynnong

Day 6: Mawlynnong to Cherrapunji
80 kms / 2.5 hrs

- After breakfast, drive to Cherrapunji, one of the wettest places on Earth . In May the entire region will be dotted with waterfalls. Your first stop will be at Nohkalikai falls, ranked high in the 100 best waterfalls in the world . This falls also has the highest plunge factor in India.
- Later we visit Seven sister falls and Wha Kabha falls.
- Your last stop would be at Mawsmai caves where you can see many unique limestone formations called stalactites and stalagmites.

O/n at La Kupar inn / Cherrapunji

Day 7: Cherrapunji

- Walk down to the Double Decker Root Bridge that has two levels. They are very strong, can carry many people at a time and perhaps can last 500 years. The trek passes through some verdant slopes, criss-crossed by streams. At some places the stream has made some natural ponds, containing emerald blue water where you can take a dip. The trek is moderately difficult since one needs to descend 2500 feet into the valley and then climb up the same altitude again. It will take approximately 4-5 hours for the up and down trip.

O/n at La Kupar inn / Cherrapunji

Day 8: Goodbye!

- After breakfast, drive down to Guwahati airport. Opt for a late evening flight as the drive from Cherrapunji to Guwahati (170 km / 5 hrs) is long and prone to traffic jams.

Location details


Meghalaya, meaning "Abode of Clouds," is a stunning state in northeastern India, known for its dramatic and varied topography. It lies on the Shillong Plateau and is flanked by Assam to the north and Bangladesh to the south. The landscape is a breathtaking mix of rolling hills, deep gorges, dense subtropical forests, and ancient limestone caves. The state is divided into three main hill regions—the Khasi, Jaintia, and Garo Hills—each with distinct cultural and geographic features. Sparkling rivers, hidden waterfalls, and living root bridges formed by nature and nurtured by local communities add to its rich ecological and geographical character.

Meghalaya experiences a subtropical highland climate, with heavy rainfall during the monsoon season, especially in Cherrapunji and Mawsynram—two of the wettest places on Earth. Summers are mild, while winters are cool and d ry. The persistent mist and moisture create a lush, green environment that defines Meghalaya’s unique monsoon charm.
