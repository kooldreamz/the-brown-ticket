Chalo Chalein Chanderi!


Chanderi, a hidden gem in Central India, is a quaint little town where time seems to have stood still since the 19th century. Stone houses merge seamlessly with fort walls and palace courtyards. The high spires of temples and many beautiful baolis play hide and seek throughout the town, and everywhere you walk, you hear the clickety-clack of handlooms weaving the finest ‘Chanderi’ silk and cotton. There is always a blur of activity in the bazaars, and as the sun sets over the majestic Chanderi Fort, the town comes alive with mesmerizing folk songs echoing from the low hillocks of the Malwa Plateau. Yet there seems to be a strange stillness in the air, bordering on the eerie!

About The Tour


Chanderi is “Not just Sarees”!. For those who think that Chanderi is a “shopping destination” meant for shopaholics, Chanderi holds a whole lot of surprises! Situated strategically on the borders of Malwa and Bundelkhand regions, this historic town has witnessed the rise and fall of empires, each leaving behind traces of their influence on its architectural marvels and cultural amalgamation.

Obviously one of the most prominent features of Chanderi is its centuries-old tradition of weaving, which has earned it a distinguished place on the global map of textiles. The town's famed Chanderi sarees, known for their sheer texture, delicate zari work, and intricate designs, are prized possessions for connoisseurs of fine craftsmanship.
But beyond its textile heritage, Chanderi boasts a wealth of architectural wonders that bear testimony to its glorious past. The imposing Chanderi Fort, perched atop a hill overlooking the town, stands as a sentinel of its erstwhile grandeur. Constructed during the medieval period by various rulers

including the Bundela Rajputs and the Mughals, the fort offers panoramic views of the surrounding landscape and provides a glimpse into the town's tumultuous history.
Exploring the narrow lanes of Chanderi's old town, one encounters an eclectic mix of Hindu, Jain, and Islamic architectural marvels, each narrating a saga of artistic brilliance and cultural syncretism. The Jama Masjid, with its elegant domes and intricately carved pillars, bears testimony to the town's multicultural ethos, while the serene tranquility of the Jain temples, adorned with elaborate frescoes and ornate sculptures, invites contemplation and reverence.

Trip highlights



Raja Rani Mahal

As the name suggests, the complex has two distinct structure. An imposing seven storey Raha Mahal (the King’s Palace) and a smaller structure called Rani Mahal (the Queen’s palace). It is a beautiful structure constructed from grey and white sandstones, and showcases large courtyards, elegant stairways, beautifully carved pillars, and open pavilions on the terrace.

Koshak Mahal

Koshak mahal is an impressive palace, and one of the most well maintained historical monuments in the town. This simple yet elegant palace from 15th century captures one’s attention for its unique architecture. Built on a square plan, the building exhibits multiple gateways with arches, offering the photographers myriad of frames to capture it with.!

Badal Mahal Gate

Badal Mahal is Chanderi’s defining monument. The Indian Postal Department issued a cancellation stamp for all posts leaving Chanderi bearing the image of the Badal Mahal Gate. This 50 feet high gate is architecturally quite an imposing structure against the backdrop of the hill fort, and bears intricate designs and netted windows.

Boodhi Chanderi

At a distance of approx. 20km from main city, amidst the dense forest and away from the habitation lies the ruins of Boodhi Chanderi. The site consists of the ruins of around 55 Jain temples, most of which are in ruins. The temples here are believed to have been built between the 9th - 11th centuries by the Pratihara kings.

Chanderi Fort

Built on the highest point of the Chandragiri Hill, the fort is a characteristic sight in Chanderi, visible from virtually every point in the town and beyond. The Fort dates back to the Mughal times, ruled by differed monarchs. It can be approached through 3 very famous gates. The main gate is known as 'Khooni Darwaza' as the criminals from battlements were thrown from the above.

Chanderi Weave

Nothing defines the town more than its Chanderi weave! Chanderi is a time-honoured ethnic textile characterized by its lightweight, sheer quality, and fine texture. Chanderi textiles are fashioned by interlacing silk, and gold zari in the conventional cotton yarn, which results in the formation of the lavish, glistening texture.

Kati Ghati

A 80 ft high and 30 ft wide gateway chiseled out of a single rock, Kati Ghati gained fame after release of the movie Stree in 2018. Like all other monuments of the town, in addition to the architecture and history, there are fascinating stories that spin around the ruler who ordered it and the mason who created it! Join us to hear this and more stories around the town.

Location Details

Chanderi is small town, largely known for Chanderi weave. Located in MP, close to the UP and MP border, it is 40 km from Lalitpur (UP) and 200+ km from city of Gwalior (MP). The town lies on the edge of the Malwa plateau at 360 metres above sea level. It is located in a trough surrounded by hills of the Vindhyachal Range that divides the Gangetic Plains of north India from central India. The Betwa River flows close to Chanderi. With a large forest cover, Chanderi is a place rich in both flora and fauna. Teak, Sal and Bamboo are the main indigenous species of the region. Chanderi has a Mediterranean, hot summer climate and pleasant winters.
